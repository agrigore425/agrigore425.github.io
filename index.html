Who I am

I gave my mother a few grey hairs as a child. I had a knack for discovering how gadgets worked. My eyes constantly searched for the next cool gizmo to cross my path. My sticky, little fingers couldn’t help but tear apart everything from watches to radios. I saw this habit as educational. My mother… not so much. But this interest in technology and electronics has carried me to where I am today.

After my mother gave me a few strict lectures, I decided to follow my interests and pursue a career in engineering. I am currently a senior at the University of Washington studying Electrical Engineering with a focus in embedded systems. Through many hours spent in lab, my friends and I have been able to complete some amazing projects. Outside of lab work, I'm involved in a student-driven research team - The UW Hyperloop team. This team is currently hard at work designing and developing a working passenger capsule to compete in a national competition hosted by SpaceX. As a member of this group, I am one of the select embedded engineers working to make this dream a reality.

Feel free to catch a glimpse of my life here at the University of Washington and view my recent work!

Autonomous and Bluetooth Enabled RoboTank

Robotank is a small tank my lab group designed to autonomously drive in confined spaces. Obstacle avoidance is implemented using distance sensor. The tank can also be controlled remotely through Bluetooth. Hardware used to complete this project included a TI Stellaris LM3S8962 board, analog distance sensors, and a tank chassis. All software, including PWMs, ADCs, UART, and drivers were all implemented in C. FreeRTOS (Real Time Operating System) was used to program the tank.
